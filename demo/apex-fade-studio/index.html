The barber
I don’t just cut.
I design.

I’m Apex — I came up doing freehand designs and never stopped. To me a head of hair is a canvas: a hard part with a clean shadow, a pattern shaved by eye, a fade that turns into a design at the temple. I cut out of Method Barbershop, downtown. Tell me what you’re picturing — the bolder the better — and I’ll make it unmistakably yours.

The craft

Cut by hand, not by stencil

A clean design isn’t a sticker. It’s drawn freehand, in the chair, around the way your hair actually grows — which is why it fits you and grows out clean.

01

The map

We start with your photo or the idea in your head, then I map it to your hairline, your part, and the canvas you’ve actually got — so it sits on your head, not a template.

02

Freehand

No stencils. Every line is cut by eye with the corner of the trimmer, the way a tattoo is drawn — which is why no two ever come out identical.

03

The shadow

Depth comes from fading the edges of the design down into the skin, so a flat pattern reads as carved — not drawn on.

04

Grows out clean

Lines are cut to soften evenly, so week two still looks intentional — never like something half-erased.
